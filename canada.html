4) Notes & Tips

Key Advice
TypeMessage
WarningMany competitive deadlines fall between November and February. Start documents early to avoid missing internal school nomination cut‑offs.
TipApply broadly — combine university entrance awards with national/provincial schemes (Vanier, OGS) and departmental RA/TA offers.
ReminderCertified translations are required if documents aren’t in English/French. Keep both digital PDFs and notarized hard copies.
TipBuild relationships with potential supervisors (for research programs) 3–6 months before deadlines; align your proposal with their current grants.
WarningStudy permit processing and biometrics can take time. Check IRCC guidance and apply as soon as you receive an offer.
ReminderEven with scholarships, proof of funds for visa may still be required (e.g., living costs not fully covered). Keep statements recent.
TipUse official portals and verify details directly: https://www.educanada.ca, https://www.univcan.ca/universities/
ReminderKeep a single folder structure for each application: 01_Transcripts, 02_Resume, 03_SOP, 04_Refs, 05_ProofOfFunds, 06_Extras.
