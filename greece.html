GREECE — Scholarship & University Guide 2026 Edition

For students from Ghana

1) Standard Documents Required (Applications to Greek Universities)

Collect and translate early (preferably January–March 2026). Use certified translations into Greek or English, as the program requires.

Document Details / Notes
Academic Certificates Senior High School certificate (for Bachelor) or Bachelor/Master degree certificate (for Master/PhD). Certified copy + official translation.
Transcripts Official transcripts with grading scale. Certified + translated.
Equivalency / Recognition (DOATAP) Some programs require recognition of prior degrees by DOATAP (Greek NARIC). Start early; processing can take weeks.
Language Proof English-taught: IELTS/TOEFL/PTE (program-specific). Greek-taught: CILS/CELI or equivalent. Check each program’s minimums.
Passport + Photos Valid passport (6+ months) and biometric photos for visa/residence permit.
Motivation Letter (SOP) Tailor to the program; show fit, goals, and why Greece.
Recommendation Letters Usually 2 (academic/professional). Use official letterhead and signatures.
Financial Proof Bank statements or official scholarship letter to demonstrate living costs coverage.
Health & Police Certificates Medical fitness certificate; police clearance (for visa).
Insurance Travel/health insurance valid in Greece (until university insurance or residence permit kicks in).
Admission/Invitation Letter Official university acceptance—mandatory for student visa (Type D).

Tip: Keep notarized copies + PDFs of everything. For printing, this page will automatically show link URLs next to each hyperlink.

3) Month-by-Month Application Timeline — 2026 Intake

Plan backward from your target start (typically Sep/Oct 2026). Key windows for EMJM and university calls are highlighted.

January 2026

Kickoff & Document Prep
  • Gather/passport, photos, transcripts, certificates; start certified translations.
  • Draft CV & Motivation Letter; line up referees.
  • Check EMJM programs and Greek university pages for 2026 calls.

February 2026

Language & Target Shortlist
  • Book IELTS/TOEFL (if needed). Confirm DOATAP (recognition) requirements for your target programs.
  • Shortlist: 1–3 EMJM programs + 2–4 Greek university programs with scholarship options.

March 2026

Application Building
  • Complete EMJM applications (many close Jan–Feb; some consortia accept until Mar).
  • Begin university applications (IHU/AUTH/UoM/AUEB). Prepare optional portfolios where relevant.

April 2026

Scholarships Window
  • Submit partial-scholarship applications (IHU/AUTH/AUEB) as their calls open.
  • Watch for IKY 2026 calls; track eligibility for foreign nationals.

May 2026

Finalize University Apps
  • Finish remaining university applications; upload all certified translations.
  • Collect updated bank statements (financial proof) if self-funding/partial funding.

June 2026

Results & Next Steps
  • Monitor offers/results; accept best option; request official Admission Letter for visa.
  • Book medical exam and police clearance for visa.

July 2026

Visa Preparation
  • Purchase travel/health insurance covering initial months in Greece.
  • Book Greek Embassy (Accra) appointment; gather visa file.

August 2026

Visa Submission
  • Submit student visa (Type D) application with Acceptance Letter and full documentation.
  • Arrange accommodation; prepare travel.

September 2026

Arrival & Residence Permit
  • Arrive in Greece; enroll; apply for residence permit as instructed by your university.
  • Open bank account if needed; activate university insurance.

October 2026

Start of Classes
  • Attend orientation; finalize course registration; confirm scholarship disbursement schedule.

4) Notes & Practical Tips

Quick rules, reminders, and best practices for Ghanaian applicants heading to Greece.

Translations & Authentication. Use certified translators; where required, legalize via the Greek Embassy/Consulate. Keep both hard copies and PDFs.
Recognition (DOATAP). Some programs need degree recognition—start this early to avoid delays.
Visa Timing. Apply 8–12 weeks before travel. Carry insurance, financial proof, acceptance letter, police & medical certificates.
Work While Studying. Check your residence permit conditions; student work is typically limited (part-time).
Where to Track New Calls. Use the national Study in Greece — Scholarships Hub, university scholarship pages, and EMJM catalogue for new 2026 postings.

Disclaimer: Scholarship availability, coverage, and deadlines can change. Always confirm on the official portals before applying.
