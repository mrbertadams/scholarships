D. Notes & Tips

  • Translations & legalization: Many documents must be officially translated (Greek/English) and legalized (Apostille or consular). Budget 3–4 weeks.
  • Visa vs. residence permit: The National (D) visa gets you into Greece; the residence permit keeps you legal for the full degree—apply soon after arrival.
  • Pick programs with funding hooks: Prioritize EMJM (full) and programs offering tuition waivers (IHU, AUEB). Use the SiG Scholarships portal to catch new calls.
  • Embassy checks: MFA bilateral calls vary by country and year. Ghana inquiries go via the Greek consular channel in Accra.
  • Ghana co-funding: Apply to the Ghana Scholarship Secretariat after you hold an offer (they require admission documents).
  • Language reality: Most Greek undergrads are Greek-taught; English-taught master’s options are expanding—verify language on each program page.
  • Keep copies: Save scans of everything (including translations) for visa/residence renewals.
  • Plan for living costs: Partial scholarships often waive tuition but not living costs—budget for rent, insurance, residence fees, and deposits.
