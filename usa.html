Notes & Tips

  • WASSCE acceptance: Many US schools accept WASSCE/WAEC English (often B3/B) for ELP; others still require TOEFL/IELTS/DET. Always confirm the school’s English Language Proficiency page.
  • Testing (2026): MIT & Harvard require SAT/ACT; Yale runs a flexible testing policy; many schools remain test-optional (e.g., University of Chicago). Verify each school’s current year page.
  • Graduate & PhD funding: Many US PhD programs include tuition + stipend via assistantships/fellowships. Department pages list specifics.
  • Self-funding paths: Consider lower-cost public universities, community-college 2+2 routes, or in-state tuition waivers where available.
  • Deadlines change: Re-check portals each autumn; some national scholarships post earlier for the following academic year.
  • Visa prep: Keep bank statements and official scholarship/aid letters ready for Form I-20 and the visa interview.
